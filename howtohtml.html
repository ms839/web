Basic Guide to HTML
HTML is the backbone to most websites on the Internet. It is a Tag-Based coding language. That means you use tags, or <>, with different words inside of the greater-than less-than symbols that tells the website what kind of text should go into it. For instance, the < p > tag tells the website that the text inside of the opening and closing tag is a paragraph.

Notice that the headings and paragraphs have closing tags, which are tags with a slash in front of the tag's text. This is a closing tag. You need a closing tag to tell the document when to end the section and start a new one. Every beginning has an end!
